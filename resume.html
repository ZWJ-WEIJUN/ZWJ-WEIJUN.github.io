Material Property Analysis of DED Thin-Walls

Multi-institutional collaboration (UC Davis, J-PARC, KIT) analyzing material properties and residual stress in DED-manufactured thin-wall specimens (2023-Present).

  • Measured internal residual stress using neutron diffractometer
  • Analyzed porosity, grain size, hardness, and tensile strength of 316L specimens
  • Demonstrated that energy-based control reduced residual stress
  • Showed powder-based control induced beneficial compressive stress
  • Established foundation for material-aware adaptive control

Comparative Study of Hybrid Additive-Subtractive Processes

Multi-institutional benchmarking study (UC Davis, UW-Madison, TU Wien) comparing three hybrid manufacturing processes: DED powder, WAAM wire, and friction surfacing (2024-2025).

  • Fabricated identical artifact geometries using three different hybrid processes
  • Assessed geometric accuracy, material behavior, and system integration differences
  • Analyzed cycle time, resource consumption, distortion, energy consumption, microstructure, and mechanical properties
  • Friction Surfacing demonstrated higher hardness due to solid-state nature
  • WAAM offered lower cycle times and resource consumption; DED provided near net shape geometries
